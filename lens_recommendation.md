### Key Findings

#### 1. **Wide-Angle Dominance (24-35mm equivalent)**
- **24mm is the most used focal length** across ALL trips
- Represents 39-46% of all photos in most trips
- **IMPORTANT:** 24mm was likely used because it was the widest available - you may prefer even wider (20mm or 16mm) if available
- Combined 24-35mm range captures **60-75% of all shots**

#### 2. **Two-Peak Distribution Pattern**
Most trips show a clear two-peak pattern:
- **Primary peak: 24mm** (wide-angle for landscapes, architecture, environmental shots)
- **Secondary peak: 75mm** (portraits, details, compression)
- Middle focal lengths (36-60mm) are used much less frequently (1-5% each)

#### 3. **Telephoto Usage (75mm+)**
- **75mm accounts for 10-16%** of photos (second most popular)
- Super-telephoto (400mm+) represents **<5%** total usage
- Wildlife/Svalbard trip shows higher telephoto usage (720mm peaks)

#### 4. **Cumulative Percentages**
Looking at cumulative distribution:
- **24-30mm covers ~50-70%** of all photos
- **24-52mm covers ~80-90%** of all photos
- **24-75mm covers ~95-99%** of all photos
